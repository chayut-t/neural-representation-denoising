\chapter*{Scope and reproducibility}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Scope and reproducibility}
This dissertation develops two computational studies and a common interpretation based on corruption and restoration. The mathematical claims are derived in the text, and the empirical claims are limited to the figures, tables, and experimental procedures presented in the relevant chapters.

The training code, model checkpoints, raw per-trial outputs, and random seeds are unavailable. Numerical comparisons are therefore reported as descriptive results rather than independent replications or claims of statistical significance. Missing implementation details and the experiments needed to resolve them are stated explicitly in \cref{sec:intro-scope,sec:synthesis-limitations}.
